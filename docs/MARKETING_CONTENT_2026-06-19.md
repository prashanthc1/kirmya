the real product (referrals, mentors, AI resume scoring, skill-gap analysis,
communities, jobs — free in MVP). **Numbers in `[brackets]` are placeholders — replace
with real metrics before publishing (Rule 9: specific = credible, but never fabricate).**

Rule key: 1 emotion→logic · 2 transformation · 3 pain>desire · 4 ride existing demand ·
5 it helps THEM · 6 ego+reassurance · 7 social proof · 8 price framing · 9 specificity ·
10 fix the offer.

These target queries laid-off professionals already type. Each: working title, target

   - Query: *just got laid off what to do*
   - Angle: emotion→logic (Rule 1); calm, reassuring, actionable.
5. **"The skill-gap method: find the 3 skills between you and your next role"**
   - Query: *how to figure out what skills I need for a job*
   - Angle: framework piece, showcases skill-gap analysis (Rule 9).

### Full draft — Post #4 (intro + structure)

> ### Laid off and panicking? Do these 5 things in the first 72 hours
>
> Getting laid off doesn't just hit your bank account — it hits your sense of who you
> are. If your stomach's been in a knot since the meeting, that's normal, and it doesn't
> mean you're behind. The first 72 hours aren't about fixing everything. They're about
> five small moves that put you back in control. *(Rule 1: name the feeling first.)*
>
> **1. Breathe and file for what's yours.** Unemployment benefits, COBRA timelines,
> final paycheck and PTO payout — write down the deadlines today so nothing lapses.
> **2. Don't mass-apply yet.** The void of job boards feels productive and rarely is.
> Referred candidates get seen first — line up *people* before postings. *(Rule 3,7)*
> **3. Get an honest read on your resume.** Most resumes are filtered by software before
> a human sees them. Score yours against the roles you want and fix the top 3 issues.
> **4. Tell ten people, specifically.** Not "I'm open to work" — "I'm targeting `[role]`
> at companies like `[X]`; know anyone there?" Specific asks get specific help. *(Rule 9)*
> **5. Talk to one person who's been here.** A mentor who recovered from the same thing
> turns panic into a plan. *(Rule 6 reassurance)*
>
> You don't have to do this alone or figure it out from scratch. Kirmya puts these
> five moves in one place — resume scoring, warm referrals, and mentors who've walked it.
> `[Start free →]`

## 5. Offer audit (Rule 10) — make traffic convert

Before buying more traffic, pressure-test the offer:
1. **Specific?** (Rule 9) Replace every "faster/better" with a real number once you have
   it — "[X]-day average to first interview" beats "get hired faster."
2. **Solves real pain?** (Rule 3) The hero leads with the layoff moment — keep it there;
   don't bury it under feature lists.
3. **Transformation clear?** (Rule 2) Every page should make the Before→After obvious in
   one glance.
4. **Proof real?** (Rule 7) Ship 3–5 genuine beta testimonials before scaling spend —
   fabricated proof destroys trust with a stressed audience and is off-limits.
